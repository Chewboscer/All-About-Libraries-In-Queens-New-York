Harnessing The Sun: Solar Projects In New York State Since 2000

Context Surrounding The Project

This project is for my advanced computer science class. We were tasked to take a large sum of data that tells a story,
and narrow it down into one macro infographic and multiple micro infographics that give the reader an easy view of what the data is showing.
For my data, I chose every solar project that has been constructed in New York State since 2000. I chose this because I am interested in
sustainable energy and I think it is crucial for the future. This data set, condensed into these infographics, help us visualize our progress in that regard over the last two decades.

Vocab

  • SOLAR PROJECTS: Construction of solar panels in a defined area by a company or group for the purpose of generating energy.
  • Kwh: Kilowatts per hour. The units for the energy production of the solar panels.

    Feedback

  • "I know its just a prototype but just make sure the colors on macropage match up. I think the annual energy production line chart is
    more relevant than developer pie chart, but maybe thats not the direction you wanna go in."

  • "On the micro page, you don't need a "developer" section, uless you can tie that in to the story, though the energy production graph
    is useful - maybe integrate energy production in to the macro view. Also, the macro color scheme is pretty distracting and not too
    informative, I would use a monochrome gradient. Since this data is pretty industry specific, I would also add some lines on the
    graphs to indicate averages, etc."

  • "This will most likely become more evident as you analyze the data (specifically the total solar projects over time), but I think
    that a link between the macro and micro pages (ex. in a larger title that summarizes both) could aid in communicating the message.
    Also, the annual energy output vs. time graph might be more beneficial to your message, unless you are focusing on specific developers
    as part of your message. I think all of the graphs are effective in their message."

    • Final Design/Takeaways From Feedback

    After considering the feedback, my final design uses a more monochrome map with different saturations of the same color.
    This makes the colors less distracting that thus aids the focus on the data. Additionally, in my initial prototype
    I was deciding between a pie chart of the developers and the line graph of the production over time, but in my final
    design I am only including the production over time. This decision was based on the feedback that the developer pie
    chart was not very relevant to my code and that the line graph was better, but also came from a personal decision
    that I felt the developer pie chart did not really carry meaning to my message about the development of solar projects.
    Who develops them is not entirely important to this data, it is the growth of their development, and that is shown in
    the amount over time and their productions.

    Data Pipeline: CSV to JSON

  • 1. I put the CSV file into a google sheets file.

  • 2. I edited the sheets file and got rid of any
    unecessary data that was not useful for my message.

  • 3. I exported the sheets file into VS code.

  • 4. In VS code, I created a format_data.py file
    where I looped through all the lines of the CSV file and
    organized what I needed into dictionaries and lists.

  • 5. After doing this, I used the format_data.py
    to create a new JSON file.
